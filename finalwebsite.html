Japanese Candlestick Patterns



Doji Candlestick

A Doji candlestick is a single candlestick pattern that signifies indecision in the market. It is characterized by a very small body where the open and close prices are nearly equal, indicating that buyers and sellers are in balance.

Dragonfly Doji Candlestick

The Dragonfly Doji is a specific type of Doji candlestick pattern that signals potential bullish reversal. It is characterized by a long lower shadow, a small body near the high of the trading range, and little to no upper shadow. This formation indicates that buyers have stepped in after a period of selling, creating a balance that could lead to a price reversal.

Gravestone Doji Candlestick

The Gravestone Doji is a distinctive candlestick pattern that signals potential bearish reversal. It is characterized by a long upper shadow, a small body at the lower end of the trading range, and little to no lower shadow. This formation indicates that buyers drove prices higher during the trading session, but sellers ultimately pushed the price back down to the opening level.

Hammer Candlestick

The Hammer candlestick is a bullish reversal pattern that typically forms after a downtrend. It indicates potential buying interest and a shift in market sentiment. The Hammer is characterized by a small body located at the upper end of the trading range, with a long lower shadow and little to no upper shadow.

Inverted Hammer Candlestick

The Inverted Hammer candlestick is a bullish reversal pattern that typically forms after a downtrend. It indicates potential buying interest and a shift in market sentiment, suggesting that buyers may start to take control.

Hanging Man Candlestick

The Hanging Man candlestick pattern is a bearish reversal pattern that typically forms after an uptrend. It indicates potential selling pressure and a shift in market sentiment, suggesting that buyers may be losing control.

Bullish Engulfing Candlestick

The Bullish Engulfing candlestick pattern is a powerful bullish reversal pattern that typically occurs at the end of a downtrend. It signals a potential shift in market sentiment from bearish to bullish, indicating that buyers are gaining strength.

Bearish Engulfing

A bearish engulfing candlestick pattern is a technical analysis indicator that signals a potential reversal from a bullish trend to a bearish trend.

Bullish Harami Candlestick

The Bullish Harami candlestick pattern is a reversal pattern that typically appears at the end of a downtrend. It indicates potential bullish sentiment, suggesting that buyers may be starting to take control after a period of selling.

Bearish Harami

A bearish harami candlestick pattern is a reversal signal that suggests a potential shift from a bullish trend to a bearish trend.

Three White Soldiers Candlestick

The "Three White Soldiers" candlestick pattern is a bullish reversal pattern that typically appears at the end of a downtrend. It consists of three consecutive long-bodied candlesticks that close higher than the previous one, signaling strong buying pressure and a potential trend reversal.

Three Black Crows

The three black crows candlestick pattern is a bearish reversal pattern that typically signals a potential shift from a bullish trend to a bearish trend. It consists of three consecutive long bearish (black or red) candles, each closing lower than the previous one.

Morning Star Candlestick

The Morning Star candlestick pattern is a bullish reversal pattern that typically appears at the bottom of a downtrend. It indicates a potential shift in market sentiment from bearish to bullish, signaling that buyers are gaining strength.

Evening Star

The evening star candlestick pattern is a bearish reversal signal that typically indicates a potential change in trend from bullish to bearish

Shooting Stars Candle

The shooting star candlestick pattern is a bearish reversal signal that typically appears at the top of an uptrend.

Three Inside Up Candlestick

The Three Inside Up candlestick pattern is a bullish reversal pattern that typically forms after a downtrend. It indicates a potential shift in market sentiment from bearish to bullish, signifying that buyers are starting to gain control.

Three Inside Down

The three inside down candlestick pattern is a bearish reversal signal that typically occurs at the top of an uptrend.

Rising Three Methods Candlestick Pattern

The Rising Three Methods candlestick pattern is a bullish continuation pattern that typically occurs during an uptrend. It indicates a brief period of consolidation before the upward movement resumes, suggesting strong bullish sentiment.

Falling Three Methods Candlestick Pattern

The Falling Three Methods candlestick pattern is a bearish continuation pattern that typically occurs during a downtrend. It indicates that the downward movement is likely to continue after a brief consolidation phase.

Spinning Top Candle

The spinning top candlestick pattern is a sign of indecision in the market and can appear in both bullish and bearish trends.

Marubozu Candle

The marubozu candlestick pattern is a strong indicator of market sentiment, typically signaling the strength of either buyers or sellers, depending on the type of marubozu formed. There are two main types: the bullish marubozu and the bearish marubozu.


Chart Patterns



Ascending Triangle Pattern

The ascending triangle pattern is a bullish continuation pattern often seen in uptrends. It indicates that buyers are gaining strength, as the price creates higher lows while maintaining a level resistance at the top.

Descending Triangle Pattern

The descending triangle pattern is a bearish continuation pattern often observed in downtrends. It indicates that sellers are gaining strength as the price forms lower highs while maintaining a level support line at the bottom.

Symmetrical Triangle Pattern

The symmetrical triangle pattern is a continuation pattern that can indicate either a bullish or bearish movement, depending on the direction of the breakout.

Bullish Pennant Pattern

The bullish pennant pattern is a continuation pattern that typically forms during an uptrend, indicating a brief consolidation before the price is likely to continue higher. It resembles a small symmetrical triangle or flag that points upward.

Bearish Pennant Pattern

The bearish pennant pattern is a continuation pattern that typically forms during a downtrend, signaling a brief consolidation before the price is likely to continue lower. It resembles a small symmetrical triangle or flag that points downward.

Rising Wedges Pattern

The rising wedge pattern is a bearish reversal pattern that typically forms during an uptrend. It indicates a potential price reversal as the price consolidates within two upward-sloping trendlines.

Falling Wedges pattern

The falling wedge pattern is a bullish reversal or continuation pattern that typically occurs during a downtrend. It indicates that the price is consolidating and can signal a potential upward breakout.

Bullish Flag Pattern

The bullish flag pattern is a continuation pattern that typically forms during an uptrend, signaling a brief consolidation before the price is likely to continue higher. It resembles a rectangle or parallelogram that tilts slightly against the prevailing trend.

Bearish Flag Pattern

The bearish flag pattern is a continuation pattern that typically forms during a downtrend, indicating a period of consolidation before the price is likely to continue lower. It resembles a rectangle or parallelogram that tilts slightly against the prevailing trend.

Head & Shoulders Top Pattern

The head and shoulders top pattern is a classic bearish reversal pattern that typically appears at the end of an uptrend. It indicates that a reversal may be imminent, suggesting that the price could decline.

Head & Shoulders Bottom Pattern

The head and shoulders bottom pattern, also known as an inverse head and shoulders, is a bullish reversal pattern typically found at the bottom of a downtrend.

Triple Top Pattern

The triple top pattern is a bearish reversal chart pattern that typically signals a potential decline after a strong uptrend. It consists of three peaks at roughly the same price level, indicating that buyers are struggling to push the price higher.

Triple Bottom Pattern

The triple bottom pattern is a bullish reversal chart pattern that indicates a potential shift from a downtrend to an uptrend. It consists of three lows (troughs) at approximately the same price level, showing that buyers are starting to gain strength.

Double Top Pattern

The double top pattern is a bearish reversal chart pattern that typically occurs after an uptrend. It indicates that the price has attempted to break through a resistance level twice but has failed, suggesting a potential shift toward a downtrend.

Double Bottom Pattern

The double bottom chart pattern is a technical analysis pattern that signals a potential reversal from a downtrend to an uptrend. Itâ€™s the inverse of the double top pattern and is often used by traders to identify bullish reversal opportunities

Bullish Rectangle Pattern

The bullish rectangle pattern is a continuation pattern that typically occurs during an uptrend. It suggests that the price is consolidating before a continuation of the upward movement.

Bearish Rectangle Pattern

The bearish rectangle pattern is a continuation pattern that typically occurs during a downtrend. It indicates a period of consolidation before a potential continuation of the downward movement.

Rounding Bottom Pattern

The rounding bottom pattern, also known as a saucer bottom, is a bullish reversal pattern that typically signals a gradual transition from a downtrend to an uptrend. It resembles a "U" shape and indicates a slow and steady accumulation phase.

Cup and Handle Pattern

The cup and handle pattern is a bullish continuation pattern that signals a potential upward movement in the price of an asset. It typically forms after an uptrend and consists of two main parts: the cup and the handle.

Diamond Top Pattern

The diamond top pattern is a bearish reversal chart pattern that typically forms at the peak of an uptrend. It resembles a diamond shape and indicates potential market exhaustion, signaling that a downward move may follow
